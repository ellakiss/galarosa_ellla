Prevalence and Impact of Teenage Pregnancy

The Philippines has one of the highest teenage pregnancy rates in Southeast Asia, with over 200,000 teenage pregnancies reported in 2019. Our website provides information and resources to help young people, parents, and caregivers understand the impact of teenage pregnancy and the challenges faced by young people in the country.

  • Challenges faced by young people in the country: Teenage pregnancy can be exacerbated by a range of factors, including:
    • Limited access to sexual and reproductive health education and services: Young people in the Philippines may face barriers to accessing sexual and reproductive health education and services, including lack of information, stigma, and discrimination. This can limit their ability to make informed decisions about their sexual and reproductive health.
    • Gender inequality: Gender inequality can perpetuate harmful gender norms and stereotypes that contribute to the prevalence of teenage pregnancy. For example, young women may be expected to prioritize family and caregiving responsibilities over education and career opportunities, which can limit their ability to make choices about their sexual and reproductive health.
    • Child marriage: Child marriage is a human rights violation that disproportionately affects young women in the Philippines. Girls who are married as children are more likely to experience teenage pregnancy, and are at greater risk of negative health and social outcomes as a result.
    • Poverty: Poverty can limit young people's access to education, healthcare, and other resources that can help prevent teenage pregnancy. Young people who live in poverty may also be more likely to engage in transactional sex or other risky behaviors that can increase their risk of pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections.

How is Pregnancy Diagnosed?

Pregnancy can be diagnosed through home pregnancy tests, readily available in most supermarkets and drugstores. These tests detect pregnancy hormones in urine and are most accurate when used about a week after a missed period. If the first test indicates a negative result, it's advisable to wait a week and take another test for confirmation. If a home test confirms pregnancy, it's essential to schedule an appointment with a doctor for confirmation through a blood test and possibly a physical examination.

What Are the Options for Pregnant Teenagers?

Teenagers facing pregnancy may feel apprehensive about seeking medical help, but it's crucial for both the mother and the unborn child's safety. Doctors discuss various options, including abortion, adoption, or raising the child independently. Involving the future father and family members in decision-making is ideal but not always feasible. Birth control clinics and public health offices offer counseling and information to assist in making the best choices.

Is it Possible for Teenagers to Have a Healthy Baby?

Teen mothers can indeed have healthy babies with proper obstetrical care. Timely medical appointments throughout the pregnancy are vital for both the mother and the baby's health. Teen mothers should adopt healthy habits, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and seeking support from friends and family. Avoiding harmful substances like cigarettes, drugs, and alcohol is crucial, as these can negatively impact the baby's development.

What Should Teenage Mothers Expect During Prenatal Visits?

Prenatal visits involve regular check-ups with the doctor to monitor both the mother and the baby's health. These visits typically increase in frequency as the pregnancy progresses. At appointments, the doctor performs various assessments, including weight measurement, blood pressure checks, and fetal monitoring. Teenage mothers should communicate openly with their doctors about any concerns or symptoms they experience.

How Can Teenage Pregnancy Be Prevented?

While abstinence is the only sure way to prevent pregnancy, various methods can reduce the risk for sexually active teenagers. Community counseling and support programs provide information on birth control methods and help teenagers understand their sexual boundaries. Peer counseling and education programs tailored to teenagers can offer valuable support and guidance.

What Birth Control Methods Are Available?

Prescription birth control methods, such as intrauterine devices (IUDs) and birth control implants, are highly effective options available through healthcare providers. Over-the-counter methods like condoms, birth control sponges, and morning-after pills are also accessible but have varying effectiveness levels. Choosing the right birth control method depends on individual preferences and medical considerations.

Conclusion

Teenage pregnancy poses challenges but with proper medical care and support, healthy outcomes are achievable. Seeking timely medical assistance, adopting healthy habits, and utilizing effective birth control methods are essential steps for teenage mothers. Accessing community resources and support programs can provide valuable assistance throughout the pregnancy journey.
